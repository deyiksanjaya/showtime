Trick #1

Thought Projection

The Effect

A spectator simply thinks of a two-digit number. After a moment of intense concentration, and without touching the phone, you "control" the stopwatch at your will. You first make it start, and then, the timer impossibly stops on the exact millisecond number that they were only thinking of.

The Secret Method

1

You must first secretly discover their number. A classic technique is to have them write it on a business card "to focus their thoughts," which you secretly peek. Let's say you peek the number 42.

2

With the phone in your hands, create a moment of misdirection. During this moment, secretly tap the World Clock tab icon.

3

Quickly enter the peeked number (42) into the hidden keypad. It will close automatically, setting your secret target. The numpad is designed to be easily accessed by your left thumb.

4

Now, demonstrate how to start the stopwatch "the layman's way": Press the Start button on the screen. Let it run for a few seconds.

5

Ask the spectator to extend their hand and imagine a "Start" button on their palm.

6

Demonstrate how to stop the stopwatch "the layman's way": Press the Stop button on the screen. The stopwatch will pause.

7

This is where the magic begins. The timer is now waiting for a delay (default is 5 seconds). The stopwatch display will remain on the time it was stopped. Pay close attention to the "World Clock" label at the bottom: One second before the timer automatically starts, the "World Clock" label will change to "World Clock." (with a period). This is your cue! When the label changes, immediately touch the spectator's palm to visually "trigger" the start. This creates the illusion that you "ran" the stopwatch from their imaginary button.

8

The timer is now running. It will automatically stop after a set duration (default is 6 seconds).

9

Again, pay attention to the timing. As the timer approaches its automatic stop, touch the spectator's palm again to visually "trigger" the stop. This time, the milliseconds will land precisely on the number you secretly set up (42), completing the illusion that their imaginary button press perfectly stopped the timer.

    Trick #2

    Mind Reading Mode

    The Effect

    A spectator creates a list of lap times, each with a seemingly random two-digit millisecond value. They silently think of just one of the millisecond numbers from the list. After a moment of concentration, you peer into their mind and reveal the exact number they are merely thinking of.

    The Secret Method

    1

    To enable this mode, secretly tap the Alarms tab icon. There is no visual change. Tapping it again disables the mode.

    2

    Hand the phone to a spectator. Instruct them to press Lap several times, and then finally press Stop to finish. This creates a list of times.

    3

    The Core Secret: The app forces the milliseconds of each lap time to be a "sum-to-nine" number (like 18, 27, 36, 45, 63, 72, 81). More importantly, it often generates them in pairs. If .27 appears, its partner .72 will also likely appear on the list.

    4

    Ask the spectator to look at the list and just think of one of the two-digit millisecond numbers. Tell them to concentrate on it.

    5

    Now, you "fish" for information. At this point you can use you favourite fishing method that you know

    6

    Because you know all the numbers are "sum-to-nine" pairs, knowing one number they are (or are not) thinking of makes it easy to deduce the others. For example, if you confirm they are not thinking of 81, you can focus your performance on revealing its partner, 18.

      Trick #3

      The Peek

      The Effect

      A spectator starts and stops the timer at a completely random time. They then press Reset, making the time vanish completely. You take the phone back, and without looking, you are able to "read their mind" and announce the exact time they stopped it at.

      The Secret Method

      1

      Press Start, Stop, and then Reset. The time will vanish.

      2

      Secretly tap the two small dots between the buttons.

      3

      The "vanished" time will flash on screen for 2 seconds for you to peek.

        Trick #4

        Be Creative!

        Effect

        The effect? You decide! With this feature, you can create your own effect using a time output from ShowTime. The sky is the limit!

        The Secret Method

        1

        Create a custom iOS Shortcut. Build any routine you can imagine in the Shortcuts app. For example, a shortcut that takes a time value and sends a pre-written text message.

        2

        Link it to ShowTime. Go to the ShowTime settings and enter the exact name of your new shortcut into the "On Press & Hold" field. Make sure the name matches perfectly!

        3

        Trigger your effect. Now, simply press and hold the Timers tab in ShowTime to run your custom shortcut and perform your unique effect.

        Important Note: The Timers button has a secret memory. Even after you press Reset and the display shows 00:00.00, ShowTime remembers the last stopped time. When you trigger a shortcut (by tapping or holding), this *remembered* time is what gets sent. This allows for powerful effects where the spectator believes the time is gone, but you can still secretly use it. This feature only works when no other special modes (like Mind Reading or Thought Projection) are active.

        Customization

        Fine-Tuning Your Performance

        Accessing Settings

        To tailor ShowTime to your unique performance style, you can access a hidden settings menu. Simply triple-tap the main time display (the 00:00.00 numbers) to open the settings page.

        Available Settings

        A

        Magic Mode Delays

        This section lets you adjust the timing of the "Thought Projection" trick. You can change the Auto-Start Delay, the Indicator Delay, and the Auto-Run Duration to match your pacing perfectly.

        B

        Timer Button Shortcuts

        Here you can change the names of the iOS Shortcuts that are triggered by the Timers tab. This allows you to integrate ShowTime with your own custom routines built in the Shortcuts app.

        C

        Reset to Default

        If you've made changes and want to go back to the original timings, just press the Reset to Default button at the bottom of the settings page.

        Handling Unwanted Situation

        Auto Switch

        This lets you create an emergency exit using iOS shortcuts. If you're in a tough spot, a single tap on the Timers tab can instantly and silently switch to the real iOS Clock app.

        How to Set It Up:

        1

        Open the Shortcuts app on your iPhone and tap + to create a new one.

        2

        Tap Add Action, search for "Go to Home Screen", and select it. This step is crucial to hide the "Back to Shortcuts" banner.

        3

        Tap the search bar at the bottom, search for "Open App", and select it.

        4

        Tap the blue "App" placeholder and choose the Clock app from the list.

        5

        Rename the shortcut to something simple (e.g., Emergency Exit) and tap Done.

        6

        In ShowTime's settings, enter that exact name (Emergency Exit) into the "On Single Tap" field and save.

        Advice

        Tips

        • Customize Your Pacing. Use the settings menu (triple-tap the time) to adjust the 'Magic Mode' delays. Match them to your speaking rhythm for a more natural and deceptive performance.
        • Master the Visual Cue. For the 'Thought Projection' trick, practice reacting precisely when the "World Clock" label adds a period. Perfect timing makes the effect truly magical.
        • Use Functions as Misdirection. Before a trick, casually demonstrate a real function, like the Lap button. This justifies you touching the screen, providing cover to secretly activate a mode.
        • Leverage the Reset. After a time is stopped, make a point of pressing 'Reset' to "erase it forever." This enhances the impact when you later reveal the time using either the two-dot peek or the secret memory feature for shortcuts.
